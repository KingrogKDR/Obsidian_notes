## Tweet 1 — Hook

What did you do today, and what constraint did you set for yourself? (e.g. "no Docker," "just raw syscalls," "manually wrote to cgroup files")

> Format: [What you avoided using] + [what you did instead] + [why]

---
## Tweet 2 — Discovery

One concrete thing you _watched happen_ — ideally with a real number, command, or output. This is the "show, don't tell" tweet.

> Format: [What you set up] → [what you watched happen] → [specific result/number]

---
## Tweet 3 — Aha

The misconception you had going in vs. what turned out to be true. This is usually the most interesting tweet — the surprise.

> Format: [What you assumed] + [what actually happened] + [why it matters]

---
## The underlying loop (works for any topic, not just this one)

**Constraint → Observation → Correction**

1. Pick something to do the "hard way" (no abstraction/tool doing it for you)
2. Watch it happen live (dashboard, logs, prints, whatever gives real-time state)
3. Notice where your mental model was wrong, and name the correction explicitly
